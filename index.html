What is Man of Sorrows?

The “Man of Sorrows” art exhibit is a collections of abstract photo images which have been created to help us to focus our attention and affections on the suffering of Christ.

Common, ordinary objects are photographed and highlighted with color which casts the objects in a new light. The highlighted images help us to see what is often overlooked.

The goal of the exhibit is see the “Man of Sorrows” in a deep and spiritual way. My prayer is that you see Christ high and exalted in His suffering.

DAY 1 | Man Of Sorrows

Jesus is the fulfillment of Isaiah’s prophetic vision. Crowned with thorns, we see the Christ humiliated and mocked. Take a moment to reflect on the suffering of Jesus. Can you connect with those who are mocking him? Christ embraces our sorrow, our shame, our mocking, our sadness, our separation, and our sin.

DAY 2 | Blood Sweat & Tears

Gethsemane is a vineyard type garden. It most likely had a olive press for crushing olives. In the garden, Christ is being pressed. His humanity is on full display. “Father, let this cup pass from me.” The agony was so intense that he was sweating blood. And so the "crushing" of the Son begins with this submission, “Not my will, but your will be done.”

DAY 3 | Not My Will

Jesus knew from the beginning that this night would come. He was fully aware that Friday was coming. I find comfort in the questions of Jesus, because they sound like so many of my questions. I am challenged to find the humility and trust to submit my life and circumstances to Him. To trust God in the most painful situations is the cup I must drink. I want to drink confidently from the cup of God’s will.

DAY 4 | Judas’ Regret

When Judas realized that Jesus was to be condemned to die, he felt condemnation and regret. He tried to take back the money, but it was too late. “I have betrayed innocent blood”, were his last recorded words. The chief priests couldn’t put the money into the treasury because it was blood money ( Glad to see they had some standards). They purchased a “potter’s field”. This is a small plot of land were broken, discarded pots were thrown. They buried Judas there. Most of us have regrets. Perhaps we have sold Jesus out for less than a few coins, but the reality of the story is Jesus will die so we will not be forgotten in a field of brokenness.

DAY 5 | WHEN THE ROOSTER CROWS

On Thursday night at Passover, Peter boasted that he would die for Jesus. Jesus shocked them all by prophesying that before the rooster crows the next morning Peter would have denied him 3 times. Crushed by the prophesy, Peter tried to prove the point in the garden by cutting off the ear of a servant.
Before dawn, Peter cursed God and declared that he never knew the Man. Peter cursed God and the he reduced Jesus to a mere man. The Man he saw walk on water, heal his mother-in-law and raise Lazarus from the dead. As he reduced Jesus from the Son of the living God To a man he didn't even know, a rooster crowed. Peter remembered and he ran away and wept bitterly.
